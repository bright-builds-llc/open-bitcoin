  `packages/open-bitcoin-consensus/src/script/sigops.rs`, and
  `packages/open-bitcoin-mempool/src/error.rs`, but confirm the final edit list
  during planning instead of promising a full-repo rewrite.
- **D-02:** Keep vendored Knots code, test-only files, low-value cosmetic
  rewrites, and all Phase 8 CLI or RPC work out of scope. The
  `AI-agent-friendly CLI surface` todo stays with Phase 8, and
  `Sweep panics and illegal states` remains a separate correctness-hardening
  phase.

### Let-else heuristics
- **D-03:** Prefer `let...else` only when a function has a focused guard-style
  extraction from `Option`, `Result`, or pattern-matched input and the `else`
  branch is an immediate `return`, `continue`, `break`, or typed error path.
  The goal is to move the happy path to the left margin, not to maximize the
  number of syntax conversions.
- **D-04:** Keep `if let`, `match`, or plain conditionals when they express
  optional side effects, ordered recognizer ladders, or genuine multi-branch
  protocol logic more clearly than `let...else`. In particular,
  `packages/open-bitcoin-wallet/src/descriptor.rs` and
  `packages/open-bitcoin-consensus/src/classify.rs` should only change if the
  rewrite is plainly easier to audit than the current ordered flow.

### Refactor shape and verification
- **D-05:** Preserve the stable module roots and public surfaces carried forward
  from Phases 07, 07.1, and 07.3. This is a function-level readability pass,
  not another module split or invariant redesign campaign.
- **D-06:** If a readability problem comes from mixed responsibilities rather
  than guard extraction, prefer a narrow helper or local reshaping over a
  forced `let...else`. New local bindings introduced during the pass should
  continue to follow the repo's `maybe_` naming guidance for optional values.
- **D-07:** Planning should begin with a search-backed candidate inventory and
  may leave clearly non-improving sites untouched; skipped candidates should be
  documented in the phase summary so the sweep stays deliberate rather than
  mechanical.
- **D-08:** Use targeted crate-level tests or checks as iteration aids where
  signing, validation, networking, or mempool behavior is touched, but the
  phase is not complete until `bash scripts/verify.sh` passes.

### the agent's Discretion
- Exact hotspot ordering, helper names, and where to stop once `let...else`

- Existing `if let` or `match` shapes may stay in place when they already

- The planner or executor may group candidate files by crate to keep the sweep
  reviewable and verification-friendly.

### Folded Todos
