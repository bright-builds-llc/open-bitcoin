## Implementation Decisions

### Scope and audit rubric
- **D-01:** Keep the phase production-only. Test-only files, vendored Knots
  code, and deferred panic or illegal-state cleanup remain out of scope.
- **D-02:** A conversion is in scope only when the current code splits a
  presence or fallibility check from the later extraction, `let...else`
  reduces indentation or removes the second lookup, and the surrounding logic
  becomes easier to read.
- **D-03:** If a candidate would require rewriting a protocol dispatch, policy
  algorithm, parser grammar, or public module boundary just to force
  `let...else`, defer it instead of editing it.

### Shortlist and default posture
- **D-04:** Audit these files first: `packages/open-bitcoin-network/src/header_store.rs`,
  `packages/open-bitcoin-node/src/network.rs`,
  `packages/open-bitcoin-wallet/src/wallet/build.rs`, and
  `packages/open-bitcoin-consensus/src/classify.rs`.
- **D-05:** Treat `packages/open-bitcoin-wallet/src/descriptor.rs` and
  `packages/open-bitcoin-wallet/src/address.rs` as default non-targets unless a
  single obvious production-only win appears during the audit.

### Adopt or defer outcomes
- **D-06:** Adopt the local `let...else` wins in
  `packages/open-bitcoin-node/src/network.rs`,
  `packages/open-bitcoin-wallet/src/wallet/build.rs`, and
  `packages/open-bitcoin-consensus/src/classify.rs`.
- **D-07:** Defer `packages/open-bitcoin-network/src/header_store.rs` because
  its remaining `if let` and tuple-building branches are already clearer than a
  forced `let...else` rewrite.

### Verification and completion
- **D-08:** Use narrow crate-scoped tests and lints while iterating, then end
  the phase on `bash scripts/verify.sh`.
- **D-09:** No public APIs, type shapes, reject reasons, or externally visible
  behaviors may change.

### the agent's Discretion
- Exact helper naming is at the agent's discretion when a tiny private helper
  makes a `let...else` conversion clearer.
- If a shortlisted file yields no additional high-confidence win during
  implementation, leave it untouched and keep the defer note.

</decisions>
